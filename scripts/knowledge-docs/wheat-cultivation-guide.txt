Wheat Cultivation Guide - Rabi Crop (India)

RECOMMENDED VARIETIES
- HD-2967: Most popular, widely adapted across North India
- PBW-343: Punjab, Haryana, Western Uttar Pradesh
- GW-322: Gujarat, Rajasthan
- K-9107: Eastern UP, Bihar, West Bengal
- HD-3086 (Pusa Malav Kiran): Heat tolerant, for late sowing

CLIMATE
- Temperature during grain filling: 12-25 degrees C
- Cool weather required during vegetative phase
- Avoid frost at booting stage
- Requires 100-200mm rainfall or equivalent irrigation

SOWING TIME
- Timely sowing (North India): 1-25 November
- Late sowing: 25 November to 10 December (use heat-tolerant varieties)
- Very late sowing: After 25 December (yield loss 0.5% per day delay)

SEED RATE
- Timely sowing: 100-125 kg/ha
- Late sowing: 125-150 kg/ha
- Seed treatment: Thiram 2.5g/kg plus Carbendazim 1g/kg

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Timely sowing (irrigated): 120 kg N, 60 kg P, 40 kg K per hectare
- Late sowing: 80 kg N, 40 kg P, 30 kg K per hectare
- Zinc deficiency common: Apply ZnSO4 25 kg/ha as basal dose
- Application: Half N plus all P plus K as basal; remaining N at first irrigation

IRRIGATION
- Critical stages: Crown Root Initiation (21 days), Tillering, Jointing, Booting, Ear emergence, Milky grain stage
- Total water requirement: 400-500mm
- Number of irrigations: 4-6 depending on soil type

COMMON DISEASES
1. Yellow Rust (Puccinia striiformis)
   - Yellow stripes on leaves parallel to midrib
   - Spreads rapidly in cool humid weather
   - Management: Propiconazole 25EC at 1ml/liter, Tebuconazole

2. Brown Rust (Puccinia recondita)
   - Orange-brown pustules randomly on leaves
   - Management: Mancozeb, Propiconazole

3. Loose Smut (Ustilago tritici)
   - Black powder replacing grain
   - Management: Seed treatment with Carboxin plus Thiram

COMMON PESTS
1. Aphids (Sitobion avenae)
   - Major pest; sucks sap from flag leaf
   - Management: Dimethoate 30EC at 2ml/liter; natural predators (ladybird beetles)

2. Army Worm
   - Damages flag leaf
   - Management: Chlorpyriphos 20EC at 2ml/liter

HARVESTING
- Harvest when moisture content is 14-18%
- Use combine harvester to reduce losses
- Average yield (irrigated): 4-5 tonnes/ha
- Maximum potential: 6-7 tonnes/ha

POST-HARVEST STORAGE
- Dry grain to 12% moisture before storage
- Use airtight bins or hermetic bags to prevent weevil damage
- Check government MSP (Minimum Support Price) before selling

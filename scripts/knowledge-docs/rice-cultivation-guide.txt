Rice (Paddy) Cultivation Guide - India

POPULAR VARIETIES
Kharif (Short Duration, 105-120 days): IR-36, Samba Mahsuri, MTU 1010
Medium Duration (125-145 days): Swarna (MTU 7029), Jaya, IR-64
Long Duration (145+ days): Pusa Basmati-1121, Sugandha, Taraori Basmati
Hybrid varieties: PHB 71, KRH-2, Arize 6444

CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 20-37 degrees C
- Rainfall: 1000-2000mm annually or equivalent irrigation
- High humidity and sunshine required

NURSERY PREPARATION
- Seed rate: 25-30 kg/ha (wet nursery), 60-80 kg/ha (dry nursery)
- Seed treatment: Soak in 1% KCl solution (float method to remove chaffy seeds)
- Treat with Carbendazim 2g/kg to prevent blast
- Raise seedlings for 25-30 days (SRI method: 8-12 days)

LAND PREPARATION
- Puddling: 2-3 ploughings with standing water
- Levelling essential for uniform water distribution
- Incorporate green manure (Dhaincha/Sesbania) before puddling

TRANSPLANTING
- Traditional spacing: 20cm x 15cm
- SRI (System of Rice Intensification): 25cm x 25cm; single seedling per hill
- Transplant at 2-3 leaf stage for SRI; 25-30 days for traditional
- Line transplanting is preferred over random transplanting

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Maintain 5-7cm water depth for first 2 weeks after transplanting
- Drain and dry field for 3-5 days at tillering (promotes root growth)
- Keep 2-5cm water at critical stages: panicle initiation, flowering, grain filling
- Drain 2 weeks before harvest
- AWD (Alternate Wetting and Drying) saves 30% water

FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- High yielding varieties (HYV): 120-150 kg N, 60 kg P, 60 kg K per hectare
- Basal: All P and K, 25% N
- Tillering (20 DAT): 50% N
- Panicle initiation (45 DAT): 25% N
- Zinc deficiency: Apply ZnSO4 25 kg/ha

WEED MANAGEMENT
- Maintain 5cm water for 2 weeks after transplanting to suppress weeds
- Bispyribac sodium (Nominee Gold) for grassy weeds at 15-20 DAT
- Pyrazosulfuron for broad-leaf weeds
- 2-4-D for sedges and broad-leaf weeds

MAJOR DISEASES
1. Blast (Pyricularia oryzae)
   - Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves; can infect neck causing panicle blast
   - Management: Tricyclazole 75WP at 6g/10 liter; Carbendazim; use resistant varieties

2. Brown Plant Hopper (BPH) - Viral vector
   - Hopper burn in severe infestation
   - Management: Imidacloprid 200SL at 0.25ml/liter; avoid excess N; drain field

3. Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB)
   - Yellow to white water-soaked lesions on leaf margins
   - Management: No effective fungicide; spray Copper oxychloride; drain standing water

4. Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani)
   - Oval lesions on leaf sheath
   - Management: Hexaconazole 5EC at 1.5ml/liter; Validamycin

MAJOR PESTS
1. Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas)
   - Dead hearts in vegetative; white ears in reproductive stage
   - Management: Release Trichogramma japonicum (1 lakh/ha); Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC at 0.3ml/liter

2. Brown Planthopper (BPH)
   - Causes hopper burn; also transmits grassy stunt virus
   - Management: Pymetrozine, Buprofezin; avoid excess nitrogen

3. Leaf Folder
   - Larvae fold leaves and feed inside
   - Management: Chlorpyriphos 20EC at 2.5ml/liter when 20% leaves are folded

HARVESTING
- Harvest when 80-85% grains are golden yellow
- Moisture content at harvest: 20-22%
- Dry to 14% moisture for safe storage
- Average yield: 4-6 tonnes/ha (HYV under irrigation)
- Basmati: 3-4 tonnes/ha

POST-HARVEST
- Threshing: Same day as harvest to minimize shattering losses
- Dry on clean surfaces; avoid drying on roads
- Store in airtight bags or PUSA bins to prevent weevil damage

Tomato Cultivation Guide - Rabi Season (India)

VARIETIES
Plains: Pusa Ruby, Pusa-120, HS-101, Punjab Chuhara
Hills: Hisar Arun, Solan Gola
Hybrid: Rashmi, Avinash-2, Naveen

CLIMATE REQUIREMENTS
- Temperature: 21-24 degrees C (day), 10-20 degrees C (night)
- Frost susceptible; avoid waterlogging
- Relative humidity: 45-65%

SOIL REQUIREMENTS
- Well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil
- pH: 6.0-7.0
- Rich in organic matter

NURSERY PREPARATION
- 400-500g seed per hectare
- Treat seeds with Thiram 2g/kg
- Sow seeds 1-2cm deep; cover lightly
- Transplant when 25-30 days old (5-6 leaf stage)

TRANSPLANTING
- Spacing: 60cm x 45cm (open field)
- Drip system: 75cm x 45cm

IRRIGATION
- Drip irrigation preferred (saves 40% water)
- Furrow irrigation: Every 5-7 days in summer, 10-12 days in winter
- Critical stages: Flowering and fruit set
- Avoid moisture stress at flowering; avoid waterlogging at any stage

FERTILIZER APPLICATION
- Basal dose: 120 kg N, 80 kg P, 60 kg K per hectare
- Top dressing: 40 kg N at 30 days; 40 kg N at fruit set
- Micronutrients: Boron 1g/liter at flowering, Calcium at fruit set

COMMON DISEASES
1. Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
   - Brown spots with concentric rings on leaves
   - Management: Mancozeb 75WP at 2g/liter, or Iprodione

2. Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
   - Water-soaked lesions, white mold on underside
   - Management: Metalaxyl-M plus Mancozeb, Cymoxanil

3. Bacterial Wilt (Ralstonia solanacearum)
   - Sudden wilting, vascular browning
   - Management: Soil drenching with Copper oxychloride; avoid waterlogging

4. Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)
   - Yellow curling leaves, stunted growth
   - Transmitted by whitefly
   - Management: Control whitefly with Imidacloprid; use resistant varieties

COMMON PESTS
1. Fruit Borer (Helicoverpa armigera)
   - Most destructive pest; larvae bore into fruits
   - Management: Pheromone traps (5/ha), Spinosad 45SC at 0.15ml/liter, NPV

2. Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)
   - Spreads TYLCV virus
   - Management: Imidacloprid 0.5ml/liter, yellow sticky traps

3. Aphids
   - Cluster on growing tips and leaves
   - Management: Dimethoate 30EC at 2ml/liter, Neem oil

HARVESTING
- First harvest: 60-90 days after transplanting
- Frequency: Every 3-4 days
- Maturity: Light red color for local market, green-pink for distant markets
- Average yield: 25-35 tonnes per hectare

ICAR Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Guidelines for Indian Crops

INTRODUCTION TO IPM
IPM is an ecosystem-based strategy focusing on long-term prevention of pests through biological control, habitat manipulation, and resistant varieties. Pesticides are used only as a last resort.

IPM PRINCIPLES
1. Prevention: Use certified disease-free seed; resistant varieties
2. Monitoring: Scouting fields regularly (economic threshold level - ETL)
3. Biological control: Conserve and release natural enemies
4. Cultural practices: Crop rotation, intercropping, sanitation
5. Chemical control: Only when ETL is exceeded; prefer selective pesticides

ECONOMIC THRESHOLD LEVELS (ETL)
Rice:
- Brown Planthopper: 10 nymphs/tiller or 20 BPH/hill
- Stem Borer: 25% dead hearts in vegetative; 10% white ears at reproductive stage
- Leaf Folder: 20% folded leaves

Wheat:
- Aphids: 25-30 aphids/ear
- Yellow Rust: First pustule appearance in field triggers action

Tomato:
- Fruit Borer: 5% fruit damage or 1 larva/plant
- Whitefly: 5 adults/leaf

Cotton:
- Bollworms: 5-8% damaged bolls

BIOLOGICAL CONTROL AGENTS
1. Trichogramma japonicum: Egg parasitoid for stem borer in rice; release 1,00,000/ha
2. Chrysoperla carnea: Predator of aphids, whitefly, thrips; release 50,000/ha
3. NPV (Nucleopolyhedrovirus): For cotton bollworm; 250 LE/ha
4. Beauveria bassiana: For whitefly, thrips; spray 5g/liter water
5. Trichoderma viride: Soil fungicide; seed treatment 4g/kg seed

PHEROMONE TRAPS
- American Bollworm: Helilure traps (5/ha)
- Fruit Borer (Tomato): Pheromone traps (5/ha)
- Change lures every 4-6 weeks
- Check traps daily and record catches

BOTANICALS AND BIOPESTICIDES
- Neem oil (1500 ppm): 3ml/liter for sucking pests
- Azadirachtin 0.03% solution for young larvae
- NSKE (Neem Seed Kernel Extract) 5% solution spray

SAFE PESTICIDE USAGE
- Always read label before use
- Use protective gear: mask, gloves, goggles, full-body suit
- Follow pre-harvest interval (PHI) strictly
- Do not mix incompatible chemicals
- Store in original container, locked away from children
- Dispose empty containers safely

PESTICIDE RESISTANCE MANAGEMENT
Rotate between different chemical groups:
- Organophosphates: Chlorpyriphos, Dimethoate
- Pyrethroids: Cypermethrin, Lambda-cyhalothrin
- Neonicotinoids: Imidacloprid, Thiamethoxam
- Diamides: Chlorantraniliprole

WATER QUALITY AND SPRAY TIMING
- Use clean water for spraying; hard water reduces efficacy
- Spray early morning or late evening (avoid noon heat)
- Wind speed below 15 km/hr for spraying
- Do not spray before rain forecast
